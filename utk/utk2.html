STEM Access: Analytic Strategy [Aspatial]

  • One logit for each PLTW® Pathway \[\frac{P_i}{1-P_i} = exp(\beta_{0} + \beta_{1}City_{i} + \beta_{2}Rural_{i} + \beta_{3}Town_{i} + \beta_{4}log(SES)_{i} + \\ \beta_{5}\sqrt{Enrollment}\;_{i} + \beta_{6}log(Prop\;Black)_{i} + \beta_{7}log(Prop\;Latinx)_{i} + \beta_{8}log(Prop\;Asian)_{i} + \alpha_{i}+\epsilon_{i})\]
    • \(P_{i}\)= Probability of offering pathway at school i
    • \(City_{i},Rural_{i},Town_{i}\)= Locale indicator variables
    • \(SES_{i}\)= Composite SES variable ranging from 0 - 999
    • \(Enrollment_{i}\)= Student school population
    • \(Prop\;Black_{i},Prop\;Latinx_{i},Prop\;Asian_{i}\)= Proportion of enrollment Black, Latinx, Asian
    • \(\alpha_{i}\)= state fixed-effects

Local STEM Teacher Learning Ecologies

Teacher Learning: Context

  • Teachers learn from four spheres — interpersonal, institutional, community, and policy1
  • Within these spheres, teachers learn with learning partners and distributed resources2
  • These partners and tools comprise teachers’ learning ecologies:

the accessed set of contexts, comprised of configurations of activities, material resources and relationships, found in co-located physical or virtual spaces that provide opportunities for learning (Barron, 2004, p.6)3

1 2 3 4 5

  1. Zavelevsky, E., & Lishchinsky, O. S. (2020). An ecological perspective of teacher retention: An emergent model. Teaching and Teacher Education, 88, 1-11.

  2. Pea, R. (1993). Practices of distributed intelligence and designed for education. In G. Salomon (Ed.), Distributed cognitions: Psychological and educational considerations (pp. 47-87). Cambridge, UK: Cambridge University Press.

  3. Barron, B. (2004). Learning ecologies for technological fluency: Gender and experience differences. Journal of Educational Computing Research, 31 (1), 1-36.

CURRENT WORK: Implications

These initial findings speak to the need for stakeholders to engage with rural/town teacher learners differently:

  • Universities should offer outreach programs and professional development opportunities in rural/town areas or nearby areas
  • State departments should provide multiple regional professional development opportunities
  • Industry should partner with rural/town teachers to provide resources and training in advanced STEM

FUTURE WORK

Research Agenda

Learning Ecosystems

  • What resources characterize regional STEM learning ecosystems?
    • How does resource availability and quality vary across communities?
    • To what extent does the regional presence of resources, institutions, and industry predict local STEM learning outcomes?
  • How does human mobility and community resource exposure shape local STEM learning outcomes?
    • How does human mobility and resource exposure vary across geographies?
  • How do student learners engage with their local STEM learning ecosystems?
    • Teacher learners?

Spatial Equity

  • Which high-leverage resources are most inequitably distributed across space?
    • How might we amplify the reach of these resources to additional geographic areas?
  • Which regions have strong STEM learning ecosystems? weaker ecosystems?
    • How might we leverage configurations of and learnings from strong ecosystems to strengthen weak ecosystems?
  • Who are the key knowledge brokers within regional spatial social networks for STEM learning?
    • How does boundary-crossing by knowledge brokers into neighboring regional networks impact STEM ecosystem health?

Data Science as Tool

  • Which ideas, skills, and practices are most important for students to learn in data science?
    • How might we support teachers in facilitating this learning?
  • What data science literacies (including ethics) are critical for students to learn in an age of AI and data science democratization?
  • How might data science ideas, skills, and practices be distributed vertically (across grade levels) and horizontally (across disciplines) in K-12 education?
  • How might we support administrators, teachers, and other stakeholders to leverage data science in improving student outcomes? community outcomes?
