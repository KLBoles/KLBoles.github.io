Spatial Inequities in Advanced STEM Success
[Student Learning = Indicator Study]

This study is focused on a student indicator - STEM Success, which I conceptualize as an indicator of access from the OTL framework. If a student enjoys success in a subject, they have had access/ opportunities to learn.

STEM Success: Literature

  • Multi-year sustained decrease in pass rates among Black, Latinx, and Native American students 1
  • Low socioeconomic status (SES) students have a pass rate 17% lower than more affluent peers 2
  • On average, men outperform women across all AP® STEM exams 3
  • In 2015 rural students had the lowest AP® STEM pass rate of all locales 4

  1. Judson, E., & Hobson, A. (2015). Growth and achievement trends of advanced placement (AP) exams in American high schools. American Secondary Education, 43(2), 59-76.

  4. Mann, S., Sponsler, B., Welch, M., & Wyatt, J. (2017). Advanced Placement across and success: How do rural schools stack up? Education Commission of the States & CollegeBoard.

  • Gender

The logit models I just showed are aspatial - meaning they didn’t incorporate where they schools were in space. W/O spatial data best we can do is categorical variables like locale and the state fixed effects.

With spatial data and spatial data science, we acknowledge that variables change across space - like across schools (points) and across counties(polygons)

First Law of Geography * Everything is related but closer things are more related than farther things

We look for this before we run a spatial model…

  • School a offers Calc..does this mean School B is more likely?

STEM Access: What is GWR?

Geographically Weighted Regression


AP® Calculus AB

  • For a given spatial unit
    • Identify neighbors within a bandwidth
    • Create spatial weight matrix (near neighbors weighted more heavily)
    • Run local regressions - 1 for each spatial unit
